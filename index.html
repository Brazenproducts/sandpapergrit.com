Choosing the right sandpaper comes down to three things: grit number, abrasive type, and whether you need wet/dry capability. Lower grit numbers (60–80) remove material aggressively, while higher numbers (220+) are for finishing. Aluminum oxide is the go-to for wood and metal; silicon carbide excels on harder surfaces like stone or ceramic. Skipping grits in your progression is the most common mistake — it leaves scratches that show up under finish.

📄 How to choose the best Best Sandpaper & Abrasive Sheets

What actually matters when buying sandpaper:

  • Grit number — Lower = more aggressive. Use 60–80 for rough shaping, 120–150 for surface prep, 220+ for final finishing. Always sand through grits progressively — skipping leaves deep scratches.
  • Abrasive type — Aluminum oxide lasts longer on wood and metal. Silicon carbide cuts harder materials like stone, ceramic, and auto body filler more effectively.
  • Wet/dry rating — Wet-rated sheets can be rinsed and reused, critical for auto body work and dust reduction. Dry-only sheets are cheaper but single-use.
  • Backing material — Paper backing (A/C/D weight) for hand sanding. Cloth or film backing for power sanders where flexibility and durability matter.
  • Pack count — Always buy more than you think you need. Sandpaper wears out faster than most people expect, especially on raw wood and metal.
